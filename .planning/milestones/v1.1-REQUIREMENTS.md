# Requirements Archive — Milestone v1.1: Structured Q/A Panel

**Shipped:** 2026-07-07
**Core Value:** When the user presses a hotkey during a meeting, a grounded, relevant AI response appears on the overlay fast enough to be useful — without ever stealing keyboard/mouse focus from the meeting app.

Turn the flat-text Q/A panel into structured, speaker-attributed cards that distinguish
questions from statements. Pulled CAP-02 (diarization) forward from v2.

## Structured Q/A (QA) — Requirements & Outcomes

- [x] **QA-01**: The transcript is captured as discrete per-speaker utterances (Deepgram diarization + utterances enabled), not one continuous text stream
  - Outcome: **Delivered (code-verified; live human-UAT deferred).** Diarization via `diarize:'true'` + `utterance_end_ms:'1000'` on the Deepgram v5 live connect; utterance boundaries from `speech_final`/`UtteranceEnd`. Automated tests pass; 2 live diarization UAT scenarios deferred at close (see STATE.md Deferred Items).
- [x] **QA-02**: Each utterance is attributed to a speaker labeled `Person 1`, `Person 2`, … and the same voice keeps the same label for the whole session (stable speaker map)
  - Outcome: **Delivered (code-verified; live human-UAT deferred).** Main-process session-scoped `SpeakerMap` maps Deepgram's drifting raw indices → stable `Person N`; reset with the transcript on Ctrl+Alt+K.
- [x] **QA-03**: Each utterance is classified as a Question or a Statement, defaulting to Statement when the classification is not confident
  - Outcome: **Delivered (code-verified).** Pure local `classifyUtterance` heuristic (punctuation `?`, interrogative openers), default Statement — no per-utterance AI call.
- [x] **QA-04**: The Q/A panel renders each utterance as its own card labeled with a sequence + speaker (e.g. `Q1 - Person 1`, `S3 - Person 2`), replacing the flat-paragraph view
  - Outcome: **Delivered & verified (5/5 must-haves; live checkpoint approved).** `reconcileFinalLog` removed; renders directly from the pushed `utterances` array.
- [x] **QA-05**: Questions and Statements are visually distinct in the Q/A panel (styling that makes questions stand out at a glance)
  - Outcome: **Delivered & verified.** Question cards get an accent stripe + tint; statements stay neutral; reinforced by the shared per-speaker color legend.
- [x] **QA-06**: The Q/A panel shows a compact list of the people identified in the session (`Person 1`, `Person 2`, …)
  - Outcome: **Delivered & verified.** Compact people row of counted colored chips (`Person 1 (12)`), undiarized `Speaker` excluded, doubling as the color legend.
- [x] **QA-07**: The utterance/speaker data flows through the existing STT provider seam (`ISttProvider`), so classification and attribution are backend-agnostic and do not couple consumers to Deepgram
  - Outcome: **Delivered (code-verified; live human-UAT deferred).** A sibling `on('utterance')` event on the seam carries speaker + classification; no consumer imports `@deepgram/sdk`.
